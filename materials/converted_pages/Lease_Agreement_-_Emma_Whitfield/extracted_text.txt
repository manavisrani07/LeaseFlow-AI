PREMISES

The Landlord agrees to lease to the Tenant the residential
premises located at 7/220 Harbour View Boulevard, Docklands
VIC 3008 (the Premises). The Premises are provided in a
furnished condition as detailed in the Furnished Items

section of this agreement. The Tenant acknowledges having
inspected the Premises prior to signing and accepts them in
their current condition, subject to the property condition

report completed at the commencement of the tenancy.

TERM AND RENT

The term of this tenancy shall commence on 1 June 2026 and
shall expire on 31 May 2027, constituting a fixed term of

12 months. Upon expiry, the tenancy may continue on a
month-to-month basis in accordance with the Residential
Tenancies Act 1997 (Vic), unless either party provides
written notice of termination as required by law.

The Tenant agrees to pay a monthly rent of $3,100.00 AUD,
payable in advance on or before the first day of each
calendar month. Rent shall be paid by electronic funds
transfer to the account nominated by the Landlord or
managing agent. Should rent remain unpaid for more than 14
days after the due date, the Landlord reserves the right to
issue a breach of duty notice and pursue remedies available
under the Acct.

BOND

The Tenant shall pay a bond of $6,200.00 AUD prior to or on
the commencement date. This bond is equivalent to two
months’ rent and will be lodged with the Residential
Tenancies Bond Authority (RTBA) in accordance with
Victorian legislation. The bond, or any remaining portion
thereof, will be refunded to the Tenant at the conclusion

of the tenancy, provided the Premises are returned in
satisfactory condition, all rent has been paid, and no
breaches of this agreement have occurred.

OCCUPANCY AND USE

The Premises shall be occupied by 1 person only, being the
Tenant named in this agreement. The Tenant must not permit
any other person to reside at the Premises without the

prior written consent of the Landlord. The Premises are to
be used exclusively as a private residential dwelling and
must not be used for any commercial, industrial, or

business purpose.

The Tenant agrees to maintain the Premises in a reasonable
state of cleanliness and repair throughout the tenancy, to
comply with all applicable laws and by-laws, and to not
cause or permit any nuisance or interference with the
reasonable peace, comfort, or privacy of neighbours.

PET POLICY

Pets are not permitted at the Premises. Building by-laws
strictly prohibit animals of any kind on the premises,
including but not limited to dogs, cats, birds, and
reptiles. The Tenant acknowledges this restriction and
agrees to comply with the body corporate’s regulations
regarding animals at all times.

PARKING

Parking is not included as part of this tenancy agreement.

The Tenant is advised that street parking and public car

parks are available in the vicinity of the Premises. Any

parking arrangements are the sole responsibility of the

Tenant and are not managed or guaranteed by the Landlord or
the property manager.

FURNISHED ITEMS

The Premises are leased in a furnished condition. A
detailed inventory of all provided furniture and appliances

is attached as Annexure A. The following items are included
at the commencement of the tenancy:

Refrigerator

Washing machine

Dryer

Sofa

Dining table with 4 chairs

Queen bed frame and mattress

Built-in wardrobes

Microwave

Dishwasher

The Tenant is responsible for the care and maintenance of
all furnished items during the tenancy. Any damage beyond
normal wear and tear may be deducted from the bond at the
conclusion of the lease.

SPECIAL CONDITIONS

The following special conditions form part of this

agreement and are binding on both parties:

Short-term rental prohibition. The Tenant must not use the
Premises for short-term rental or subletting purposes,
including listing the Premises on platforms such as Airbnb,
Stayz, or any similar accommodation service. Any breach of
this condition shall constitute a serious breach of the
tenancy agreement.

Building amenities. The Tenant is entitled to use the
building’s shared amenities, including the gym, swimming
pool, and rooftop terrace, subject to the body corporate
rules and regulations as amended from time to time. Access
to these facilities is provided at no additional cost but

may be restricted or revoked in accordance with body
corporate procedures.

OBLIGATIONS

Landlord obligations. The Landlord shall ensure the
Premises are maintained in good repair and in a condition
fit for habitation. The Landlord shall comply with all
obligations under the Residential Tenancies Act 1997 (Vic),
including providing quiet enjoyment of the Premises,
maintaining the property in a reasonably secure condition,
attending to urgent repairs within the timeframes
prescribed by law, and ensuring all fixtures and fittings
are in good working order at the commencement of the
tenancy.

Tenant obligations. The Tenant shall pay rent punctually,

keep the Premises reasonably clean, avoid causing damage to
the property, promptly notify the Landlord or property
manager of any defects or necessary repairs, and not make
alterations to the Premises without prior written consent.
The Tenant shall allow reasonable access to the Landlord or
their agent for inspections, repairs, and valuations, in
accordance with the notice requirements set out in the Act.
End of tenancy. Upon expiry or termination of this
agreement, the Tenant shall vacate the Premises and return
all keys and access devices. The Premises must be left in a
condition consistent with normal wear and tear, having
regard to the property condition report prepared at the
commencement of the tenancy. A final inspection will be
arranged by the property manager.
